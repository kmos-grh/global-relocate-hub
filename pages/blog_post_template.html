How long does a full move really take?

7 min read – GRH Editorial Team

What actually causes delays.

Moving internationally is one of life's most significant undertakings, yet many people underestimate the time it truly takes from start to finish. While a domestic move might take a few weeks to plan and execute, international relocations often require 8-12 weeks of careful coordination.

The complexity goes far beyond simply packing boxes. Documentation, visa processing, housing searches, and customs clearance all add layers of time that catch many movers off guard. Understanding these timelines upfront can save you stress, money, and help you plan more effectively.

"Most people think moving internationally takes 4-6 weeks. In reality, the planning phase alone should start 10-12 weeks before your move date to avoid costly rush fees and last-minute stress."

— Sarah Mitchell, International Moving Specialist

Breaking Down the Timeline

Every international move is unique, but understanding the typical phases helps you plan more effectively. Here's what the process typically looks like:

Weeks 10-12: Initial Planning

Research your destination country's requirements, begin visa applications, and start gathering important documents. This is also when you should begin researching international moving companies and getting initial quotes.

Weeks 8-10: Documentation & Bookings

Finalize your moving company selection, complete visa applications, and begin the housing search in your destination country. Start notifying banks, insurance companies, and other service providers about your move.

Weeks 4-6: Preparation Intensive

Begin serious decluttering and packing preparation. Complete medical checkups, school transfers, and start the customs documentation process. This is often the most stressful phase as multiple deadlines converge.

Weeks 1-2: Final Sprint

Complete packing, handle last-minute documentation issues, and manage the actual shipping and travel logistics. Many people are surprised by how much work remains in these final weeks.

Common Delay Factors

  • • Visa processing delays (can add 2-8 weeks)
  • • Housing market constraints in destination country
  • • Customs documentation issues
  • • School enrollment and transcript transfers
  • • Pet relocation requirements and quarantine periods

The key to a smooth international move is starting early and building buffer time into your timeline. While 8-12 weeks might seem excessive, it allows you to handle unexpected delays without derailing your entire plan.

Remember, rushing an international move almost always costs more money and creates unnecessary stress. Give yourself the time you need, use professional services where appropriate, and don't hesitate to lean on the expertise of verified relocation partners who understand the complexities involved.
